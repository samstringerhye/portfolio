# Samsung.com Redesign

Working across a massive cross-functional team on a global platform, I contributed to three areas of Samsung.com: the design system, page layouts, and the global navigation bar. The GNB was the hardest problem — simplifying a navigation that had to work across 12+ markets without losing discoverability.

  ## Design System

  I contributed atoms and components to Samsung's design system — building pieces that regional teams could compose into market-specific pages without breaking brand consistency. ## The Problem

Samsung.com's navigation had accumulated complexity from years of market-by-market additions. Different product catalogues meant different category hierarchies. Different languages meant different string lengths and overflow behaviours. Right-to-left scripts flipped the entire spatial logic. The existing GNB had grown organically and it showed — every market had its own workarounds. The challenge was designing a navigation architecture strict enough to read as one brand globally, but flexible enough that no market needed to break it.

</ProseGroup>

## The Work

As a Senior Designer at Razorfish, my role was fluid across the project. I designed GNB variations and prototyped them, built page layouts to Samsung's brand standards, and contributed design system atoms and components. For the GNB specifically, every iteration had to be stress-tested against the full range of script directions, catalogue depths, and nav item counts — not just a single locale. Most of the design work was about complexity reduction: the navigation had too many levels and options, and the challenge was simplifying without losing discoverability.

## What I Learned

The GNB problem was information architecture before it was visual design. Designing for the hardest case first — the most complex market's catalogue depth and script direction as the baseline — forced decisions about hierarchy, truncation, and overflow that a single-locale design would never surface. At global scale, the designer's job is to write constraints regional teams can work within, not to design every variant. A system that requires exceptions is a system that will drift.
