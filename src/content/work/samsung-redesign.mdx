# Samsung.com Redesign

On a massive cross-functional team, I contributed to three areas of Samsung.com: the design system, page layouts, and the global navigation bar. The GNB was the hardest problem. Simplifying a navigation that had to work across 12+ markets without losing discoverability.

  ## Design System

  I contributed atoms and components to Samsung's design system: pieces that regional teams could compose into market-specific pages without breaking brand consistency. ## The Problem

Samsung.com's navigation had accumulated complexity from years of market-by-market additions. Different catalogues meant different category hierarchies. Different languages meant different string lengths and overflow behaviours. Right-to-left scripts flipped the entire spatial logic. The GNB had grown organically and it showed: every market had its own workarounds. The challenge was a navigation strict enough to read as one brand globally, but flexible enough that no market needed to break it.

</ProseGroup>

## The Work

As a Senior Designer at Razorfish, my role was fluid. I designed GNB variations and prototyped them, built page layouts to Samsung's brand standards, and contributed design system atoms and components. Every GNB iteration had to be stress-tested against script directions, catalogue depths, and nav item counts across locales. Most of the work was complexity reduction: too many levels and options, and the challenge was simplifying without losing discoverability.

## What I Learned

The GNB problem was information architecture before it was visual design. Designing for the hardest case first (the most complex market's catalogue depth and script direction as baseline) forced decisions about hierarchy, truncation, and overflow that a single-locale design would never surface. At global scale, the job is to write constraints regional teams can work within, not to design every variant. A system that requires exceptions will drift.

</ProseGroup>
