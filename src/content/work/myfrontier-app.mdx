# MyFrontier App Redesign

The MyFrontier app had fallen behind on usability, brand, and the kind of craft that makes a utility app feel like it was built with intention. The redesign addressed all of it. Cleaner interface, better navigation, and motion that made the thing feel alive.

## Approach

As ADD, I owned layout design across multiple screens and defined visual hierarchy for core UI components like the menu, calendar, and selectors. I also drove the motion design system. Motion wasn't decorative here. It was doing real UX work, bringing consistency and clarity to interactions that previously felt arbitrary.

### Motion Design

Motion gave users immediate feedback that their actions went through. A coin animation confirmed a payment, a leaf marked a completed setup, and the intro sequence covered the initial load so the app felt responsive from the first tap.
